Who we are

Luxury-yacht surveying, with the ocean as our client too.

We are independent marine surveyors who specialize in the vessels most people only dream of — and we believe protecting a yacht and protecting the sea are the same discipline.

From a 40-foot cruiser to a 60-meter superyacht, our surveys blend old-world craftsmanship knowledge with modern diagnostics: thermal imaging, ultrasonic thickness, moisture mapping, and engine-oil analysis. Every report is written to be read by underwriters, yards, and owners alike.

  • Truly impartial. No conflicts of interest — just findings.
  • Conservation baked in. We flag anti-fouling, discharge, and emissions issues others overlook.
  • Photograph-grade reports. Detailed, defensible, delivered fast.

How it works

A calm, transparent process from first call to final report

No jargon, no surprises. Here's exactly what happens when you engage us.

Tell us about the vessel

Share the make, model, LOA, year, and location. We send a fixed, all-in quote within hours — no travel padding, no hidden fees.

We survey on-site

Hauled-out inspection, moisture & thermal scan, machinery run, and a supervised sea trial. Typically one to two days on location.

Receive a defensible report

A bound, photograph-documented report with findings prioritized, fair market valuation, and recommended next steps — promptly after the survey.

Negotiate with confidence

Use our findings to renegotiate, walk away, or proceed. We remain on call for your broker, insurer, or yard.

What makes us different

The only survey firm that treats the ocean as a second client.

Every vessel we survey is also assessed for its environmental footprint — because a yacht that's sound for its owner should be sound for the waters it sails.

  • Environmental Health Check on every report. Anti-fouling chemistry, black-water systems, fuel & oil containment, and emissions.
  • Hybrid & electric expertise. From solar-assisted cats to full battery-electric drivetrains.
  • Bottom-paint & discharge focus. We flag the hull, bilge, and sanitation issues that leach into the water.
